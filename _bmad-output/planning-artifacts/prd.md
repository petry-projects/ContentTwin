# Content Twin — Product Requirements Document

**Author:** Donpetry
**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Project Type:** SaaS B2B | **Domain:** Marketing AI / Social Media Automation | **Complexity:** Medium | **Context:** Greenfield

## Executive Summary

Small, mission-driven organizations — non-profits, community groups, local associations — generate incredible authentic content daily (food rescues, hive inspections, volunteer events) but lack the capacity to turn those moments into a consistent social media presence. DIY posting is unsustainable, agency outsourcing costs $400-800/month for mediocre results, and neglect means missed volunteer recruitment, lost donor engagement, and communities underserved.

The Agentic Marketing Ecosystem (Content Twin) is an AI-powered Social Media Agent that lives inside the communication channels organizations already use — WhatsApp, Slack, GroupMe — and autonomously transforms authentic, real-world content into consistent, on-brand social media campaigns across Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and X. Volunteers share photos and updates in their group chat as they always have. The agent ingests that content, applies a machine-readable Brand DNA profile (mission, tone, visual identity), and produces platform-optimized posts — scheduled for engagement, aligned with calendars and trends, and indistinguishable from what the organization's best communicator would write with unlimited time.

Target users: Executive Directors who need social media "handled" without thinking about it (Director Ellen), tech-savvy board advisors who need a configure-once solution for multiple organizations (Board Member Brian), volunteers who contribute content passively with zero behavior change (Volunteer Val), and scaling creators who have great content but no time to post consistently (Charley Ann, DJ). The platform saves 10-20 hours/month at $49-149/month — 3-10x cheaper than agency alternatives with better quality and consistency.

### What Makes This Special

**Authentic-first architecture.** Every competitor either generates content from scratch (producing AI slop — 54% negative sentiment, 73% of companies report generic-sounding output) or provides scheduling tools that still require a human marketer. The Content Twin occupies the only empty quadrant in the competitive landscape: HIGH AUTONOMY + HIGH AUTHENTICITY. Content starts with real photos, videos, and stories from the field — not AI-generated from nothing.

**Zero behavior change required.** The agent plugs into WhatsApp and group chats where organizations already communicate. No new app to learn, no workflow to adopt. Volunteers may not even know the agent exists.

**Machine-readable Brand DNA.** Not a style guide written for humans — a structured YAML profile covering mission, tone, values, visual identity, and "this-but-not-that" brand boundaries, purpose-built for AI interpretation. This prevents the brand voice drift that plagues 73% of companies using AI content tools.

**Accumulated intelligence moat.** Every piece of content processed, every approval/rejection, every brand voice refinement deepens the system's understanding. Prompts are portable; brand DNA training is not. Switching costs grow with usage, creating natural retention.

**Self-promoting product.** The Content Twin promotes itself — ingesting its own development updates, user testimonials, and feature announcements to generate cross-platform marketing content. Both a validation mechanism and a cost-zero customer acquisition channel.

The $5.65B AI-in-social-media market (37.1% CAGR to $70.53B by 2034), mature technology stack (Claude SDK, MCP, LoRA video), and 90% reduction in LLM API costs create a 12-18 month window for category definition.

## Innovation & Competitive Landscape

### Novel Patterns

**1. Authentic-First Content Pipeline (Category-Defining)**
No existing platform ingests raw, authentic content from messaging channels as the primary content source. The WhatsApp-to-multi-platform pipeline is a genuine white space — the first system that bridges "where authentic moments happen" and "where audiences live."

**2. Machine-Readable Brand DNA (YAML)**
A structured, machine-interpretable brand identity specification covering mission tone, visual identity markers, auditory DNA, and "this-but-not-that" boundaries. Purpose-built for AI interpretation — solves the brand voice drift problem (73% of companies) at the architectural level.

**3. MCP-Native Agentic Orchestration (The Conductor)**
Built on the Model Context Protocol (10,000+ active servers, donated to the Agentic AI Foundation). Each capability is an MCP server or API/SDK integration, not a monolithic feature. New platforms become plugins, not rewrites. ZX Kane Social-Agents validated this approach for 3 platforms; the Content Twin extends it to the full marketing pipeline.

**4. Self-Promoting Product (Recursive GTM)**
The Content Twin promotes itself from day one — both a validation mechanism (if it can't market itself, it can't market others) and a cost-zero customer acquisition channel.

### Competitive Positioning

- **Legacy tools** (Hootsuite, Buffer, Sprout Social): LOW AUTONOMY, moderate authenticity
- **AI agent platforms** (NoimosAI, ActiveCampaign): HIGH AUTONOMY, LOW authenticity
- **Content Twin**: HIGH AUTONOMY + HIGH AUTHENTICITY — the only empty quadrant

The market is highly fragmented with no single vendor owning >15% across all tiers. The fragmentation IS the opportunity — the first full-stack, authentic-first platform defines the category.

## User Journeys

### Journey 1: Director Ellen — "I Forgot About Social Media (And It's Never Been Better)"

*Ellen Bowen, founder of Food Rescue US-Miami. 200+ food donors, 1,200 volunteer rescuers, 80 agencies. Recovered 12M+ lbs of food from landfills.*

**Opening Scene:** It's 6:47am and Ellen is already coordinating a food rescue from Whole Foods. Her phone buzzes — three volunteers confirming pickups, two agencies requesting deliveries, and a donor asking about tax receipts. She hasn't posted on Instagram in 11 days. The last agency she hired ($600/month) posted generic content that sounded nothing like FRUS. She canceled after 3 months. There's a Super Bowl rescue event in two weeks that could recruit 50 new volunteers — if anyone knew about it.

**Rising Action:** Board Member Brian sets up the Content Twin for FRUS-Miami in 25 minutes. The agent reviews FRUS's Instagram history — 847 posts over 6 years — and extracts a draft Brand DNA: grassroots urgency, community warmth, impact-forward storytelling, never preachy. Brian runs the Brand Interview with Ellen during a 15-minute phone call. "Your tone is passionate and action-oriented, but NOT corporate or guilt-tripping." "You celebrate the volunteers, not the organization." "You always lead with pounds rescued, not dollars raised." The agent produces 5 sample posts from last week's WhatsApp group chat photos. Ellen reviews them. Four out of five sound exactly like her. She edits one caption — "we say 'food rescue' not 'food recovery'" — and the agent learns.

**Climax:** Friday morning. Ellen has been running rescues since 5am. She hasn't thought about social media once all week. At lunch, she opens Instagram on impulse. There's a post from yesterday's Whole Foods rescue — the photo Val shared in the WhatsApp group at 7:15am, now a polished carousel with "2,400 lbs rescued from one store in one morning" as the hook, volunteer photos in slides 2-4, and a CTA: "Join our next rescue — link in bio." It has 3x more engagement than anything Ellen ever posted manually. She didn't create it. She didn't review it. It sounds exactly like her.

**Resolution:** Month 3. Ellen's social media is "handled." Posting is 5-7x per week across Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok (up from sporadic 1-2/week). Volunteer sign-ups are up 40% — people see the rescues happening in real time and want in. A local news station found FRUS through a viral TikTok the agent created from a Super Bowl rescue video. Ellen spends zero hours per week on social media. She spends those hours on her mission.

**Requirements revealed:** WhatsApp ingestion, Brand DNA extraction + interview, content transformation (photo → carousel), cross-platform publishing, autonomous posting mode, feedback learning ("food rescue not food recovery"), weekly digest.

### Journey 6: System Operations — "The Pipeline That Runs Itself"

*The Content Twin platform itself, monitored by the founding team during MVP.*

**Opening Scene:** It's 2am. Nobody is awake. The Content Twin is processing content from 15 organizations across 3 time zones. The system needs to process all of this autonomously, reliably, and correctly.

**Rising Action:** The orchestration layer monitors all connected WhatsApp groups. New content arrives → classify (post-worthy vs. chatter) → apply Brand DNA YAML → generate platform-specific variants → schedule for optimal engagement → queue for review or auto-publish per org configuration.

A failure occurs: Meta's Content API returns a rate limit error for FeedBHM's Instagram post. The system retries with exponential backoff. Second attempt succeeds. Logged. No human intervention needed.

A different failure: WhatsApp's API returns an authentication error for Grace Klein's group. The system detects the auth failure, pauses ingestion for that org, and sends an alert to Brian: "Grace Klein's WhatsApp connection needs re-authentication." Brian reconnects in 30 seconds the next morning.

**Climax:** Weekly ops review. Dashboard shows: 97 posts published across 15 organizations. 99.2% delivery success rate. 2 retried API calls, both successful. 1 auth reconnection required. Average content-to-published latency: 8 minutes. Brand Voice Fidelity Score: 84%. Zero content rejected post-hoc.

**Resolution:** The system scales from 15 to 50 organizations with no architecture changes. The plugin model means adding a new platform is a new integration, not a rewrite. Failures are self-healing by default and only escalate when human action is genuinely required.

**Requirements revealed:** Autonomous pipeline processing, retry/backoff for API failures, authentication monitoring and re-auth flow, admin alerting for actionable failures only, ops dashboard, multi-org scaling, plugin extensibility.

The MVP must demonstrate the full "magic moment": a volunteer shares a photo in WhatsApp, and an on-brand post appears on Instagram without anyone creating it.

**Resource model:** Solo founder using agentic development (Claude Code, MCP toolchain). Key constraint: founder time, not engineering headcount.

## SaaS Platform Requirements

### Tenant Model

**MVP:**
- Each organization/creator is an isolated tenant with its own Brand DNA YAML, connected social accounts, WhatsApp group, content queue, and publishing schedule
- Full tenant isolation — no cross-tenant data visibility
- Advisors switch between org contexts (no unified multi-org dashboard for MVP)
- Tenant provisioning: self-service signup → Brand DNA onboarding → WhatsApp connection → active

**Post-MVP:** Advisor dashboard, agency/multi-tenant management, org-level analytics and billing.

### Permission Model

**MVP (WhatsApp-Native):**
- The designated WhatsApp group serves as both the content ingestion source AND the permission boundary
- Anyone in the Admin Group can submit content, approve/reject queued posts, and provide feedback
- WhatsApp's native Group Admin role controls membership — the Content Twin inherits this rather than building custom RBAC
- No separate login/dashboard required for MVP — all interaction through WhatsApp

This works because non-profits and small orgs already manage group membership in WhatsApp, and adding a separate permission system creates adoption friction.

**Post-MVP:** Web-based review dashboard, role differentiation (Admin/Reviewer/Viewer), SSO, audit trail.

### Subscription Tiers

| Tier | Price | Target | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pilot** | Free (donated) | 3-5 non-profits | Full feature access, direct support, feedback partnership |
| **Creator** | $49-99/month | Solopreneurs, niche experts | 1 brand, 4 platforms, WhatsApp ingestion, 30 posts/month |
| **Organization** | $99-149/month | Non-profits, associations | 1 brand, 4 platforms, WhatsApp ingestion, unlimited posts, weekly digest |

**Post-MVP:** Growth ($149-299/month), Enterprise ($999-2,000+/month).

### Integration Architecture

**MVP Integrations:**

| Integration | Direction | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| WhatsApp Business API | Inbound | Content ingestion from group chats |

**Architecture principle:** MCP servers preferred, but API/SDK/CLI integrations supported where MCP is not practical. Adding a new platform means adding a new integration plugin, not modifying core logic.

### Technical Architecture

**Deployment:** Cloud-hosted service. GCP likely, but evaluating solution-native hosting (n8n or similar). Final decision deferred to architecture phase.

**Orchestration:** Central pipeline coordinates: ingest → classify → transform → schedule → publish. Each stage is an independent integration with defined inputs/outputs. n8n emerging as "Agentic MCP Hub" for long-running pipeline tasks. Composio for OAuth complexity management.

**Data:** Brand DNA YAML per-tenant as single source of truth. Content queue with state management (ingested → classified → transformed → scheduled → published). Feedback loop storage for Brand DNA refinement. Cloud storage + CDN for media assets.

**Decisions deferred to architecture phase:** Final hosting platform, database selection, media processing toolchain, CI/CD approach.

## Functional Requirements

### Onboarding & Brand Discovery

- **FR1:** System can autonomously analyze an organization's existing social media accounts (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, X) to extract posting patterns, visual style, caption tone, hashtag usage, and engagement data
- **FR2:** System can generate a draft Brand DNA profile from the agentic media review analysis
- **FR3:** Organization leader or advisor can participate in a guided Brand Interview conversation to confirm, correct, and refine the draft Brand DNA
- **FR4:** System can capture "this-but-not-that" brand boundaries during the Brand Interview (e.g., "warm but NOT corporate," "food rescue not food recovery")
- **FR5:** System can generate a machine-readable Brand DNA YAML from the combined media review and interview inputs
- **FR6:** Organization leader or advisor can connect social media accounts (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, X) during onboarding
- **FR7:** Organization leader or advisor can designate a WhatsApp group as the content ingestion source
- **FR8:** System can generate sample posts from recent WhatsApp group content for leader review during onboarding
- **FR9:** Advisor can set up and onboard multiple organizations from a single account by switching between org contexts

### Content Ingestion

- **FR10:** System can monitor a designated WhatsApp group for incoming messages in real time
- **FR11:** System can ingest photos, videos, voice notes, and text messages from the WhatsApp group
- **FR12:** System can classify incoming messages as post-worthy content vs. operational chatter
- **FR13:** System can extract context from message content (caption text, who shared it, timestamp, media type)
- **FR14:** System can handle multi-contributor ingestion (many group members sharing content in the same group)
- **FR15:** System can ingest content from WhatsApp self-chat (creator ingestion pattern for solo users like Charley Ann)

### Content Transformation

- **FR16:** System can generate platform-optimized captions in the organization's voice per Brand DNA YAML
- **FR17:** System can adapt a single piece of content into platform-native formats for Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and X
- **FR18:** System can format photos for each platform's aspect ratio and create carousel layouts from multiple images
- **FR19:** System can process video content — identify key moments, trim to optimal length per platform, add text overlays/captions
- **FR20:** System can generate hashtags appropriate to the content, platform, and organization's brand
- **FR21:** System can generate interactive content types (polls, questions) when appropriate to the content
- **FR22:** System can incorporate calls-to-action aligned with the organization's mission (volunteer, donate, attend, purchase)
- **FR23:** System can create recurring content series from patterns in ingested content (e.g., "Beginner Win of the Week," "Tuesday Rescue Report")
- **FR24:** System can cross-reference content with calendar events (holidays, awareness months, local events) for contextual relevance

### Publishing & Scheduling

- **FR25:** System can publish content to Instagram (posts, carousels, Reels, Stories)
- **FR26:** System can publish content to Facebook (posts, Stories)
- **FR27:** System can publish content to TikTok (videos, photos)
- **FR28:** System can publish content to X (tweets, threads, media posts)
- **FR29:** System can schedule posts based on optimal engagement windows per platform
- **FR30:** System can maintain a target posting frequency of 4-7 posts per week per organization across platforms
- **FR31:** System can retry failed publish attempts with exponential backoff
- **FR32:** System can continue publishing to other platforms if one platform's API fails

### Review & Approval

- **FR40:** System can evolve Brand DNA over time based on accumulated approval/rejection feedback

- **FR42:** Organization leader can update Brand DNA preferences through conversation (new vocabulary, updated tone, changed mission emphasis)

## Non-Functional Requirements

### Performance

- **NFR1:** Content ingestion-to-queue latency: ≤15 minutes (standard content) or ≤5 minutes (urgent content — e.g., "happening now," "need help," time-sensitive calls to action)
- **NFR2:** Content transformation latency: Queued content → platform-ready posts within 10 minutes
- **NFR3:** End-to-end pipeline latency: WhatsApp share → published post within 30 minutes (autonomous mode)
- **NFR4:** Brand Interview response time: ≤10 seconds per agent response
- **NFR5:** Agentic Media Review: Complete within 10 minutes for accounts with ≤5,000 posts
- **NFR6:** Sample post generation: 3-5 posts within 4 minutes during onboarding

### Security

- **NFR7:** All API tokens and WhatsApp credentials encrypted at rest and in transit
- **NFR8:** Per-tenant data isolation enforced at application and data layer — no cross-tenant data leakage
- **NFR9:** OAuth tokens stored securely with automatic refresh and expiration handling
- **NFR10:** WhatsApp group content accessible only to the designated tenant's pipeline
- **NFR11:** Brand DNA YAML files treated as confidential IP — not accessible to other tenants
- **NFR12:** No hardcoded secrets; all credentials via environment variables or secret manager

### Scalability

- **NFR13:** MVP architecture supports 25 concurrent organizations without performance degradation
- **NFR14:** Scales to 200+ organizations with horizontal scaling (no architectural rewrite)
- **NFR15:** Plugin architecture allows new platform integrations without modifying core pipeline
- **NFR16:** Content queue handles burst ingestion (50+ photos during a single event) without message loss
- **NFR17:** Publishing pipeline supports 500+ posts/week across all organizations at growth phase

### Reliability

- **NFR18:** Content pipeline uptime: ≥99.5% (≤3.6 hours unplanned downtime per month)
- **NFR19:** Zero content loss: All ingested content persisted before processing
- **NFR20:** Platform API failures handled with retry + exponential backoff; self-healing for transient errors
- **NFR21:** Authentication failures detected within 5 minutes; admin notified; ingestion paused (not lost)
- **NFR22:** Graceful degradation: one platform's failure does not affect other platforms or other organizations

### Integration

- **NFR23:** All integrations use standardized input/output contracts — MCP servers preferred, API/SDK/CLI supported where MCP is not practical
- **NFR24:** WhatsApp integration supports all media types: photos, videos, voice notes, text, documents
- **NFR25:** Meta integration supports all Instagram content types: single image, carousel, Reel, Story
- **NFR26:** Platform API rate limits respected with intelligent throttling — no API bans
- **NFR27:** OAuth token lifecycle managed automatically: initial auth, refresh, expiration warning, re-auth prompt

### Compliance & Content Safety

- **NFR28:** System does not generate content violating platform terms of service
- **NFR29:** Content safety filtering applied pre-publication — no hate speech, misinformation, or harmful material
- **NFR30:** AI-generated content labeled per platform requirements (EU AI Act readiness for post-MVP)
- **NFR31:** Organization's WhatsApp content used only for authorized social media publishing — no secondary use, resale, or training on other tenants' data
